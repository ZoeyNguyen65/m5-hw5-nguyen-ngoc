About

Background:

Herbert Bayer was born in 1900 in Haag, Austria-Hungary (now Austria) and passed away in 1985 in Montecito, California, USA. He was a well-known Austrian-American artist, typographer, and graphic designer who had a big impact on the growth of modern design. With his early years spent in Austria and his latter years in the United States, where he continued to create and have an impact on the design world until his death, Bayer's life and work stretched across numerous nations and continents.

Beginning and ideas:

  • Studied at the Bauhaus school in Weimar, Germany (1921-1925)
  • Influenced by Bauhaus principles and modernist movements
  • Focused on functional, simplified, and universal design

Key Artworks

  1. "Bayer Universal" font (1925, later renamed "DIN 1451")
  2. "fon" magazine (1925-1930, co-founder and designer)
  3. "Bauhaus Exhibition" Poster (1923)
  4. "World Geo-Graphic Atlas" (1953)
